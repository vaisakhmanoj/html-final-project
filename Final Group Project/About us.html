About us

The first Sony Walkman, the TPS-L2, was introduced in July 1979. It was a portable cassette player that allowed people to listen to music on the go. The device was designed by Sony co-founded Masaru Ibuka and was initially met with skepticism, but it quickly gained popularity. The Walkman had a significant impact on the music industry and how people consumed music. It provided a more personalized and portable way to listen to music compared to traditional home-based audio systems.

Our History

Sony was founded on May 7, 1946, by Masaru Ibuka and Akio Morita. The company started as an electronics shop called Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo (Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation). Its initial focus was on repairing electronic equipment and developing new products. In 1958, the company officially changed its name to Sony Corporation. The name "Sony" is a combination of two words: "sonus," the Latin word for sound, and "sonny," a colloquial term used in the 1950s to refer to a young boy. Sony introduced its first tape recorder, the G-Type recorder, in 1950. This marked the beginning of Sony's involvement in the recording and playback industry.One of Sony's most iconic products, the Walkman, was introduced in 1979. It transformed the way people listened to music by providing a portable and personal audio experience.

Expanding across the world

Sony expanded its business into the entertainment industry by acquiring Columbia Pictures in 1989 and CBS Records in 1988. The company established itself as a major player in the global entertainment market.

Sony Walkman products have a global presence, with distribution and sales extending across various channels worldwide. These portable audio devices are prominently featured in electronics retailers, both physical stores and online platforms, making them accessible to consumers in numerous countries. Sony's official stores and showrooms, strategically located in major cities globally, offer customers the opportunity to experience Walkman products firsthand and receive assistance from knowledgeable Sony staff. The company also engages in global marketing campaigns, utilizing diverse media channels and collaborations to enhance the Walkman brand's visibility on an international scale. Sony's commitment to innovation is reflected in its Walkman line, where the company continues to introduce new models with advanced features, ensuring its relevance and competitiveness in the ever-evolving portable audio market.
